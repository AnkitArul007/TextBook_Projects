FY Budget 2022-2023

Budget 2022 focuses on growth boosting high multiplayer capital, infra spending

The government has allocated Rs, 2.1 trillion for food subsidy and Rs. 730 billion for the MNREGA programme. While both these amounts
are expectedly lower than the FY2022 levels, it will likely raise the allocation on these accounts if warranted during then course of then year.

The government of India has only pencilled in a modest total spending growth of 4.6 percent for FY2023, over a 7.4% growth in FY2022.
However, it has budgeted for a much higher capex expansion of 24.5% in FY2023, with enlarged allocations of roads and railways, in
addition to a sharp increase in assistance to states for capital expenditure.

The most important part of the budget of FY2022 is the huge spending on Defence system of our country.The government
has also equally kept their attention towards the agricultural field as it is the back bone of a nation like India.
